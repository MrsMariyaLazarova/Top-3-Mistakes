Top 3 Mistakes That Can Aggravate a Fungal Infection

The new normal forces us to spend more time at home and keeps us away from healthcare centers. More and more people rely on ineffective home remedies and practices to prevent skin irritation, neglecting the side effects that it may cause. Too often, when it comes to fungal infection of the feet and nails, the incorrectly diagnosed condition is being handled the wrong way at home.

The current situation is getting even more complicated - the infections lead to poor immunity, which makes us even more vulnerable to health ailments. Therefore, it is essential not to over-rely on home self-prevention and to avoid the following mistakes.

Mistake #1: Overuse of baking soda, vinegar and other everyday products

Nearly 90% of people with fungal infection have tried “pantry staples” on their skin, usually baking soda and vinegar. Most of them have trusted ads or articles touting the advantages of foot soaking in miraculous solutions made at home. Unfortunately, for most of those who did it (73%) this led to an aggravation of the infection, and for the rest - it simply did not work.

Make sure that a particular substance is harmless. For example, vinegar, considered a remedy for infections, is not only of doubtful effectiveness but it also comes with a slew of health risks and side effects such as unpleasant smell, burns, inflammation and atopic dermatitis.

Mistake #2: Footbaths with mouthwash and sanitizers

Studies show that during the pandemic, people of all ages use sanitizers and mouthwashes to avoid infections of any kind - even foot fungus. Most mouthwash products contain Thymol and Eucalyptol, which very effectively deal with irritations and inflammation… only when it comes to ailments in the oral cavity and teeth.

Be aware of the proper use of hygiene products. This year general practitioners noted an extraordinary increase in cases of hygiene products misuse. Using hand sanitizer for foot bathing may cause the skin to dry out, which does not remove the fungus but spreads it throughout the skin.

Mistake #3: Poor Diet

71% of people suffering from foot fungus have tried some sort of diet to make the foot fungus go away, but only 2% of them stated that it proved effective. However, all respondents have experienced stomach pain, metabolic disorders and dietetic problems due to the malnutrition.

Do not put your health at risk. Of course, keeping a balanced diet that excludes sugar, starch, yeast and alcohol, improves the overall health. But consuming a lot of garlic, for example, will likely do nothing against the foot fungus, although it can cause you stomach ache.
